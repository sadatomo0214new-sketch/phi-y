Φ–Y Dock V16-03: Compression, Stem, and Branching

A pressure-sensitive redefinition of spring-time

0. One-Line Core

V16-03 redefines time-effect as a pressure-sensitive spring structure: phase θ returns, height z does not, same-phase points form a Y-axis stem, and near C_s = 0 a single critical gate opens vertical access, projection readability, pressure-drive reversibility, and non-unique re-entry branching as simultaneous faces of one event.

Japanese core: V16-03は時間効果を、圧に応じて伸縮するバネ構造として再定義する。位相θは戻るが高さzは戻らず、同位相点はY軸の串を作る。そしてC_s=0近傍では、一つの臨界ゲートが開き、垂直アクセス・投影可読化・圧駆動反転・戻り写像の非一意分岐が、一つのイベントの複数の顔として同時に立つ。

1. Purpose

V16-01 defined the Multi-Scale Time Engine as mechanism.

V16-02 defined where that engine becomes readable: the projection port, where the Y-axis stem pierces a reference frame.

V16-03 asks what happens when the spring structure is compressed.

V16-01: engine / gear-coupled time-effect
V16-02: projection port / readable cut
V16-03: compression / stem / critical gate / branching

The central task is to separate four things that must not be confused:

scale amplification
pressure-driven compression
boundary-contact synchronization
chaotic branching

2. Minimal Axioms

The structure begins with two minimal conditions:

θ returns.
z does not return.

Formally, for a period T:

θ(t + T) ≡ θ(t) mod 2π
z(t + T) > z(t)

This is enough to produce same-phase vertical stacking.

The period value itself is not the essence. Seconds, minutes, hours, days, or any other recurring frame can instantiate the same structure if phase returns while height accumulates.

3. The Stem Without Fixed Period

For a given phase θ₀, define the same-phase stem:

S_{θ₀} = { (θ₀, z_n) | n ∈ Z }

This is the Y-axis stem.

It does not depend on a privileged period such as seven days, one day, one hour, or one second.

Any periodic frame system with irreversible stacking gives a stem.

The stem exists wherever phase returns and height does not.

4. Spring Compression C_s

Let the distance between adjacent frames be:

d_n = z_{n+1} - z_n

Let d₀ be the normal or reference frame distance.

Define spring compression ratio:

C_s = d_n / d₀

Interpretation:

C_sReading
C_s = 1Normal frame spacing.
0 < C_s < 1Compression.
C_s → 0Inter-frame collision / critical gate neighborhood.
C_s > 1Extension.

Important boundary:

C_s is not immediately identified with label-depth C or concavity C.

C_s is a spring compression ratio. The relation between C_s and earlier C-language remains open.

5. Pressure Origin and Drive Direction

V16-01 used a clean base engine:

micro → macro

V16-03 generalizes drive direction by pressure origin.

Define a provisional pressure sign:

σ_p = sign(p_in - p_out)
ConditionReading
σ_p > 0Internal pressure dominant. Macro-first drive may become possible.
σ_p < 0External pressure dominant. Micro-first clean gear drive remains dominant.
σ_p = 0Critical balance. Not passive waiting, but maximum branching / spike potential.

The earlier reading of σ_p = 0 as equilibrium or waiting is rejected for Part 3.

σ_p = 0 is not the point where motion dies. It is the point where the drive can jump.

6. The C=0 Gate

C=0 is not a magic point.

It is treated as the opening of a critical gate:

G(C_s)

where:

G(C_s) ≈ 0   when C_s is safely above 0
G(C_s) ≈ 1   when C_s → 0

When this gate opens, five faces become possible together:

  1. Vertical access through the Y-axis stem.
  2. Thorn visibility.
  3. Projection-port readability.
  4. Drive-direction reversibility.
  5. Non-unique re-entry / branching.

These are not five unrelated events.

They are five readings of one gate-opening event.

7. Separating λ-Amplification from Butterfly Branching

V16-01 used:

λ_macro = λ_micro^N

This is radial scale amplification.

It describes radius, thickness, and conical expansion.

It is not yet chaos.

Butterfly branching requires a different condition:

the return map ceases to be unique

Normal gear mode:

θ_{n+1} = F(θ_n)

C_s = 0 neighborhood:

θ_{n+1} ∈ F_C(θ_n)

where:

F_C(θ_n) = { F_1(θ_n), F_2(θ_n), ..., F_k(θ_n) }

Equivalently:

F_C = F_0              if G(C_s) ≈ 0
F_C = {F_1, ..., F_k}  if G(C_s) ≈ 1

Butterfly enters not through radial scale amplification, but through non-unique re-entry mapping near C_s = 0.

However, this bracket also introduces a boundary-contact problem: it places inter-frame contact and inner/outer pressure balance under the same gate without yet proving whether they are one boundary event or two synchronized boundary events.

9. Pressure as Projection Problem

The pressure term remains unresolved.

At this stage, pressure is treated primarily as a projection-dependent structure.

Self-reference pressure and inter-frame compression pressure are not immediately defined as two separate substances.

They are first read as different projection components of a still-undefined pressure structure.

ProjectionPressure reading
Top projectionIntra-frame tension in the (r, θ) plane: phase accumulation, radial tension, inward/outward bias of circular motion.
Side projectionInter-frame compression along the z-axis: push/pull transmitted through gear coupling, stem penetration, and scale-to-scale transfer.

Provisional notation:

P = unresolved pressure structure
P_top  = Π_top(P)
P_side = Π_side(P)

where:

P_top  ≈ self-reference pressure reading
P_side ≈ inter-frame compression pressure reading

Whether these two readings are components of one pressure field, or partially independent pressure systems, remains open.

10. Boundary Problem: Inter-Frame and Inner/Outer Contact

The compression gate also exposes a boundary problem.

There are at least two boundary readings in V16-03.

10.1 Inter-frame boundary

The first boundary lies between adjacent frames:

d_n = z_{n+1} - z_n
C_s = d_n / d_0

This is the z-direction boundary.

It reads whether Frame_n and Frame_{n+1} are separated or in contact.

ConditionBoundary reading
C_s = 1Normal separation.
C_s → 0Inter-frame contact / boundary collapse.
C_s > 1Extension / increased separation.

In the language of contact, C_s measures whether temporal frames are attached or separated.

10.2 Inner/outer pressure boundary

The second boundary lies between internal and external pressure:

σ_p = sign(p_in - p_out)

This is the inner/outer boundary.

It reads whether internal pressure or external pressure dominates.

ConditionBoundary reading
σ_p > 0Internal pressure dominant.
σ_p < 0External pressure dominant.
σ_p = 0Inner/outer pressure boundary reaches critical balance.

At σ_p = 0, the inner/outer distinction is not erased as doctrine, but it becomes maximally unstable as drive direction.

σ_p = 0 is the point where inner/outer pressure balance can become spike potential.

10.3 The unresolved contact joint

The sharpest boundary question is not only the inter-frame boundary, nor only the inner/outer pressure boundary.

It is the joint where these two boundary readings are placed under one gate:

G(C_s) · [ δ σ_p ∇L_pressure + η Ξ_branch ]

This bracket synchronizes two forms of contact:

  1. z-direction contact: Frame_n and Frame_{n+1} approach contact as C_s → 0.
  2. inner/outer contact: p_in and p_out approach critical balance as σ_p → 0.

The current model does not yet prove whether these are two projections of one boundary structure or two partially independent boundary systems synchronized by the gate.

The boundary problem is therefore not only “which boundary touches?” but whether the two contact readings are themselves attached.

This is the boundary-language version of the unresolved pressure problem.

11. Remaining Thorn: What Is Pressure Applied To?

This is the central unresolved thorn of V16-03.

Earlier Y-axis pressure referred to pressure on self-reference.

The spring model introduces pressure on inter-frame distance.

The boundary formulation rewrites this as:

P_side = pressure on inter-frame boundary
P_top  = pressure on inner/outer or self-reference boundary

These may be coupled.

They may be different projection components of one pressure structure.

They may also be partially independent systems.

The relation between self-reference pressure, inter-frame compression pressure, and their boundary-contact readings remains open.

This distinction must not be closed prematurely.

12. What This Page Does Not Claim

V16-03 does not claim:

  1. C_s is identical to earlier label-depth C.
  2. λ-amplification is the same as chaotic branching.
  3. p_in and p_out are already physically defined.
  4. Self-reference pressure and inter-frame compression pressure are identical.
  5. The inter-frame boundary and inner/outer boundary are proven to be one boundary.
  6. The central equation candidate is final.
  7. C=0 is a magic point where anything can happen.
  8. All spike events are chaotic branching events.

It claims something narrower:

When spring compression approaches C_s = 0, a single critical gate can open vertical access, projection readability, drive reversibility, and non-unique re-entry branching as synchronized faces of one event, while leaving the identity or independence of the underlying boundaries unresolved.
