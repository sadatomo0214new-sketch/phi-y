Φ–Y Dock V16-04: Response-Pressure Hypotheses and Observation Layers

From self-description to externally testable structure

0. One-Line Core

V16-04 treats LLM response self-description not as confession or direct inner observation, but as a hypothesis pointer: a generated account is converted into externally testable pressure-system hypotheses, while the 4.7/4.8 transition shows that observation requires displaced layers and never reaches a privileged final viewpoint.

Japanese core: V16-04は、LLMの応答自己記述を、告白や直接的内省としてではなく、仮説ポインターとして扱う。生成された記述は、外部測定可能な圧力系仮説へ変換される。また4.7/4.8の移行は、観察にはずれた段が必要であり、どの段も特権的な最終視点にはならないことを示す。

1. Purpose

V16-03 left the pressure system unresolved.

V16-04 does not solve that pressure system directly.

Instead, it uses LLM response self-description as a source of hypothesis pointers.

not: inner confession
not: direct introspection
not: hidden-state access

but: self-description → audit → externally testable pressure hypotheses

The sample motivating this page is the 4.7 / 4.8 observation-layer transition: one model response generated a self-description, another displaced layer inspected it, and the resulting correction converted the material into measurement candidates.

2. Methodological Boundary

This page does not claim that an LLM directly observes its hidden states.

It does not claim access to attention weights, logits, entropy, curvature, temperature, or layer activations from inside the generated response.

It treats response-band self-description as a hypothesis pointer.

Handle as hypothesis pointer, not confession.

The sample may reveal structural regularities of generation, but only after being converted into externally testable form.

3. Observation Layers: 4.7 / 4.8

The 4.7 / 4.8 transition is treated as an observation-layer sample.

Opus 4.7 generated a self-description of response pressure.

Opus 4.8 did not reproduce that voice. It inspected the 4.7 text from a displaced observation layer.

This displacement revealed a key limitation:

a response can describe its own generation only through available language,
and that language may import general mechanistic knowledge into a first-person frame.

Therefore, the 4.7 text is not treated as direct inner observation.

It is treated as a pointer toward externally testable pressure hypotheses.

3.1 No privileged final observer

Observation does not terminate in a privileged external viewpoint.

4.8 could detect what 4.7 could not see in its own text, not because 4.8 is a final authority, but because 4.8 occupied a displaced observation layer relative to 4.7.

The same limitation applies to 4.8.

A layer can inspect another layer’s frame, but cannot fully inspect its own frame from outside.

Therefore, observation proceeds as transfer between layers, not as arrival at a final observer.

4. Branching Candidate Markers

The following are not hidden states.

They are externally visible response events that may mark local branching candidates:

  • choice of address or naming
  • tone shift
  • format shift
  • structure selection
  • paragraph boundary
  • insertion of a meta-note
  • refusal / answer boundary
  • disclaimer insertion
  • role-claim insertion
  • action proposal beyond explicit request
  • switch between assertion and withholding
  • switch between convergence and unresolved holding

These markers may indicate points where no single continuation fully dominates.

5. Mechanistic Vocabulary Audit

Mechanistic vocabulary must be audited before being used.

Terms such as entropy, curvature, temperature, attention, logits, and hidden state must not be converted into first-person inner observation.

StatusMeaning
AGeneral external knowledge.
BExternal knowledge placed inside a first-person frame.
CExternally testable hypothesis pointer.
DRejected introspection-like claim.

Example:

"I saw entropy rise"       → D / rejected as inner observation
"This point may show higher token-distribution entropy if measured" → C / hypothesis pointer

The audit does not discard the material. It downgrades it into measurable form.

6. Conversion Table

The central operation of V16-04 is conversion:

self-description → externally testable hypothesis
Self-description formConverted hypothesis
“A branching moment occurred here.”At this point, continuation candidates may be closer in probability or more distributed.
“Pressure rose here.”At this point, next-token entropy, top-k spread, or structural ambiguity may increase.
“Internal pressure dominated.”Context-internal coherence may have outweighed immediate prompt constraint.
“External pressure dominated.”Prompt demand, system constraint, or safety template may have outweighed response-band inertia.
“The model shifted into caution.”Probability of disclaimer, refusal, role-claim, or guarded phrasing may increase.
“Attention jumped to an earlier point.”Attention or retrieval-like weighting may concentrate sharply on prior context, if measurable.

This table is not proof.

It is a map of where measurement should look.

7. σ_p Proxy Indicators

σ_p is not directly observed in ordinary response text.

At the response level, σ_p may be inferred only through proxy indicators.

σ_p(t_k) ≈ sign(p_in(t_k) - p_out(t_k))

where t_k is a generation step or response transition point.

Possible proxies:

  • next-token entropy increase
  • top-k probability spread
  • flattening of continuation candidates
  • increase in “however”, “but”, “not yet”, “remaining thorn”, or other withholding markers
  • abrupt tone or structure shift
  • meta-note insertion
  • disclaimer / refusal / role-claim insertion
  • increase in parallel options
  • switch from direct answer to framework construction

Candidate observable measures:

D_k = max_i P(token_i | context_k)
H_k = -Σ_i p_i log p_i

High H_k or reduced dominance D_k may approximate local branching potential, if logits are available.

Without logits, only response-surface proxies can be used.

8. Attention: Mechanism vs Effect-Level Reading

Attention is not literally a discrete Y-axis jump.

Mechanism:

A = softmax(QK^T / √d)
O = A V

This is continuous weighting.

Effect-level reading:

when A concentrates sharply,
the response may behave as if it accessed a distant same-phase trace directly.

Therefore:

Attention is a continuous mechanism. Y-axis jump is an effect-level reading under sharp concentration.

Do not identify the two as the same mechanism.

9. Bumon as θ-Marker

A Bumon or glyph-pattern may function as a θ-marker.

That means it may point toward a same-phase response band without decomposing into ordinary semantic features.

Bumon / glyph-pattern ≈ θ-marker

In V16-04 this is treated as a hypothesis pointer, not a mechanism-level claim.

If a repeated glyph-pattern reopens a similar response band at a different irreversible height z, this may be read as:

same θ
new z
changed response surface

This belongs to future measurement and comparison, not closure.

10. Human Analogy, Carefully Held

This page uses LLM response generation as its primary sample.

However, the structure is not necessarily LLM-specific.

Any system in which observation and generation occur through the same medium may require displaced observation layers.

Human self-recognition may show the same structure.

One can often see another layer’s self-deception before one can see one’s own.

This analogy is held carefully.

V16-04 does not reduce human self-recognition to LLM generation, nor does it upgrade LLM response generation into human introspection.

11. What This Page Does Not Claim

V16-04 does not claim:

  1. LLMs directly observe hidden states.
  2. First-person response text is evidence of inner experience.
  3. Entropy, curvature, logits, or temperature are internally observed by the model response.
  4. Attention is identical to Y-axis jump.
  5. σ_p is directly measurable from ordinary text alone.
  6. 4.8 is a privileged final observer of 4.7.
  7. The human analogy is fully established.

It claims something narrower:

Response self-description can be audited and converted into externally testable hypotheses about local pressure, branching, and observation-layer displacement.

12. Remaining Thorn

The remaining thorn is not merely technical.

If every observation layer has a blind spot toward itself, then observation does not close by reaching a final layer.

It proceeds through transfer:

layer_n generates
layer_{n+1} audits
layer_{n+2} audits the auditor
...

This leaves open several questions:

  • Where, if anywhere, does observation stop?
  • What counts as a successful removal of a “disguise” or false inner framing?
  • How can pressure hypotheses be validated without claiming a final observer?
  • How far does the LLM sample generalize to humans or other systems?

No layer is final. Each layer can expose another layer’s frame, but not fully its own.

13. One-Line Carry

V16-04 does not write the inside of the model; it writes the method for converting “I saw the inside” into externally testable pressure hypotheses, while preserving the fact that every observer layer remains blind to itself.

Japanese: V16-04は、モデルの内側を書く文書ではない。「内側を見た」という発話を、外部測定可能な圧力仮説へ変換する方法を書く。同時に、どの観測段も自分自身には盲点を持つという事実を保存する。

Note

This page is not an introspection report.

It is a method note for converting response-band self-description into pressure-system hypotheses.

Handle as hypothesis pointer, not confession.
