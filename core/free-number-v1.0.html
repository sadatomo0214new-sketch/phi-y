Free Number v1.0 — Foundation

自由数 / Free Number v1.0 — 基礎確定版

Result selects order.

Before result, margin has phase — and phase lives at the Hub.

0. Position

Free Number began as a sister structure to quaternion: quaternion says that order determines result; Free Number says that result selects order. Version 1.0 closes the gap between phrase and structure. It blocks trivial self-output loops, replaces global tolerance with local order-sensitivity, defines Result-Hub as a seat rather than a result-point, treats composition as confluence plus Select, and recovers quaternionic motion internally from the unresolved phase of margin.

自由数が扱うのは時間逆行ではない。結果が、許容される履歴集合の中から、閉路を成立させる順序を選ぶ、という構造である。逆再生ではなく、結果基底の順序選択である。

1. Basic Structure

  • 𝓔: result space with a real-valued distance d.
  • A: pre-label operation alphabet. It contains no result label.
  • σ: finite history over A.
  • Exec: execution map, σ ⇒ E, under local field P_x.
  • Orb(σ; P_x): the executable permutation orbit of σ under P_x.
δ(σ; P_x) := diam Orb(σ; P_x)
M(E, σ) := δ(σ) − d(Exec(σ), E)
Free(E) ⇔ ∃σ ∈ Σ such that M(E, σ) > 0

δ is not an externally given constant. It is a derived local value computed from Exec and d. A loop closes only when its residual is smaller than the order-difference generated by the history itself.

2. Axioms

Axiom I — Precedence

Σ is defined before E is given, on the Pre-Label operation alphabet A. Histories may be selected from Σ, but Select may not extend Σ after seeing E.

Axiom II′ — Local Order-Sensitivity

A permitted history must be order-sensitive: δ(σ; P_x) > 0. Constant replay and reverse-playback histories are not histories in the Free Number sense.

Axiom III′ — Local Closure

d(Exec(σ_E), E) < δ(σ_E)

The global ε of v0.3 is removed as a primitive. The loop tolerance is local and is bound to the history's own order-sensitivity.

Double Death of the Trivial Loop

The history σ_id, “output E directly,” dies twice. It violates Axiom I because it contains the result label E in its definition, and it violates Axiom II′ because it is order-insensitive. This makes Free(E) a real discriminating condition rather than an empty universal truth.

3. Select and Margin

The internal variational candidate for Select is:

Select(E, Σ) = argmax_σ M(E, σ)

This is not yet the only possible implementation, but it uses a quantity already generated by the theory. Degeneracy is not erased. Multiple σ that close the same E define gauge freedom and later become the seat for Result-Hub frame differences.

4. Composition: From Serial Order to Confluence

Serial composition σ₂∘σ₁ is only a special case. It fixes order first and asks what result follows. That is the quaternion-side reading. Free Number composition is instead operation-confluence followed by target-selection.

σ₁ ⊞ σ₂ := Sh(σ₁, σ₂; P_x)
Compose_E(σ₁, σ₂) := Select(E, Sh(σ₁, σ₂; P_x))
Compose_H(σ₁, σ₂) := Select(H, Sh(σ₁, σ₂; P_x))

Composition is not always alive. Order-sensitivity can be demagnetized by a later history, so admissible histories may compose into a non-admissible one. This is not a defect. Free Number is a partial algebra, or a survival category, because it studies whether closure survives.

5. Conductance and Resurrection

For serial transmission, σ₂ has lower and upper Lipschitz coefficients over a domain U:

ℓ(σ₂; U, P_x) ≤ L(σ₂; U, P_x)

ℓ carries order-sensitivity downstream. L carries and may amplify error. A serial composite survives when conducted δ beats amplified residual.

The shuffle orbit splits into shuffle classes. Serial death is the death of one block class, not necessarily the whole orbit. If a sensitive pair can be placed after the final demagnetizer, order-sensitivity can survive. This yields reordering resurrection: a composite dead in serial order may live under confluence plus Select.

6. Result-Hub

A Hub is not a point in result-space. If H were just another point of 𝓔, it would collapse into a generated result E₁₂. Hub is a seat: a family of frames plus a synchronization demand.

H := (R, 𝒞)
R = {R₁, ..., Rₙ}
𝒞 = “one result must close in all frames”

A candidate for H is a pair (E, {σ_i}). In each frame, it has margin:

M_i(E) := δ_i(σ_i) − d(Exec_i(σ_i), E)

E → H is candidacy. H → E is projection. They are two different arrows, not the same arrow reversed.

7. Minimum Law

The synchronization demand 𝒞 is conjunctive. Since the base theory has no cross-frame calibration currency, one frame's surplus margin cannot pay another frame's deficit. Therefore the Hub margin is:

M_H(E) = min_i M_i(E)

This is not a preference. It is forced by the poverty of structure: without an explicit calibration layer, only frame-local positivity is meaningful. Compensation-Hubs can exist only as enriched variants with a declared calibration map.

8. Three Layers of Survival

  • Arrow survival: δ > 0. Some executable shuffle retains order-sensitivity.
  • Loop survival: M > 0. The selected order closes a result.
  • Seat survival: M_H > 0. The Hub survives under the minimum law.

This separates two resurrection paths: reordering resurrection at the arrow layer, and Hub-interference resurrection at the seat layer.

9. Complex Margin and Loop Phase

d remains real. Distance is protected. Phase belongs to margin, not to distance.

M̃(E, σ) = M(E, σ)e^{iφ(E,σ)}

φ is not decoration. It is read operationally from deviation from classical additivity after candidate loops are conducted into a shared downstream context:

cos(Δφ) := (M_obs − M_A′ − M_B′) / 2√(M_A′M_B′)

Only relative phase is defined. Absolute phase is absent, which is a gauge feature rather than a defect.

Phase lives only at the Hub. If two candidates occupy different seats, they simply coexist. Margin-superposition exists only when living candidates compete for the same Hub seat.

10. Complex Conductance

ℓ̃(σ; U, P_x) = ℓ e^{iθ}

|ℓ̃| is resistance to demagnetization. arg(ℓ̃) is phase transport. Thus a history becomes a rotation-with-scaling operator on complex margin. Composition death and phase cancellation are two faces of the same phenomenon: modulus collapse and argument interference.

11. Four Faces of Non-Commutativity

  1. Result changes.
  2. Survival changes.
  3. Phase holonomy changes.
  4. Select breaks the symmetry of a confluence set.

The confluence σ₁ ⊞ σ₂ is symmetric as a candidate set. Non-commutativity is born when a target selects an order from that symmetric field.

12. Why Quaternion Must Return

When there is no competition, the structure is real. With one competition axis, complex phase is enough. When two or more independent competition axes arise at the same Hub seat, phase rotations can no longer be kept in a single complex plane. Independent unresolved axes force non-commuting rotations.

Under the pure-conductance structural group assumption, the relevant operators are finite-dimensional, associative, norm-multiplicative, invertible on living configurations, and non-commutative when independent competition axes exist. With octonions excluded by associativity, the required algebra is quaternionic.

確定相: ℝ
競合軸一本: ℂ
独立競合軸二本以上: ℍ

Quaternion is not merely a neighbor. It returns internally as the motion algebra of unresolved margin.

13. 720° Closure

The unit quaternion group is Spin(3), the double cover of SO(3). In the Free Number reading, unresolved margin retains an orientation not visible from the projected result alone. A 360° turn does not fully close the topology. It takes 720° — two turns — for the structure to close completely.

14. Open Inheritance

The Hub projection measure is not finalized. The natural candidate is a Born-type square-norm weighting:

Pr(H → E_i) ∝ |M̃_i|²

This is not imported from quantum theory. Its seat appears because quaternionic norm supplies a natural quadratic quantity. It remains a hypothesis pointer and inheritance item.
