Quantum Observation as Pre-Label Geometry

Reordering the Quantum Observer Problem

Do not place the observer first. Read from irreversible labeling.

0. Purpose

This document does not replace the physical description of quantum mechanics.

It reverses the usual order in which observer, collapse, basis selection, record, and past are treated as prior entities, and rearranges them as pre-label signal, band, irreversible projection, record, and retrospective role-name.

This is not an alternative physical process theory. It is an attempt to reorganize the conceptual deadlock of the observer problem as a problem of ordering under Pre-Label Geometry.

1. Wavefunction

The wavefunction is treated here as a mathematical object capable of describing a pre-label possibility structure.

This document does not decide whether the wavefunction is a physical entity or an abstract calculation object.

The minimum condition required for connection with Y-axis theory is that the wavefunction can describe a structure that has not yet been labeled.

2. C=0: Zero Label-Depth Holding Point

C=0 is not an instant on the physical time axis. It is an epistemic point of zero label-depth.

It may have width in physical time.

The instantaneous projection postulate, the finite duration of decoherence, and Y-axis C=0 can coexist as descriptions on different axes.

C=0 does not mean “when.” It means “at what label-depth.”

3. Y-axis: Epistemic Vertical Axis

The Y-axis is not an observed object.

It is an epistemic geometry for reading observation, basis selection, labeling, and irreversible projection.

The wavefunction is a state-description on the object side. The Y-axis is a coordinate system for reading how that state falls into observation relation, result label, and retrospective observer-name.

Therefore, the wavefunction and the Y-axis are not short-circuited.

4. Basis Selection: Falling into a Band

In the physical description, interaction with the environment stabilizes a particular basis. This belongs to the language of decoherence and einselection.

In the Y-axis description, bands pre-exist in label-space, and interaction draws the signal into one of those bands.

These two layers coexist. Y-axis theory does not deny environmental basis selection; it reads its epistemic side as falling into a band.

Physically, the basis is stabilized by interaction. Epistemically, the signal falls into a band.

5. Wavepacket Collapse

The physical process of wavepacket collapse is left to the existing quantum-mechanical descriptions.

On top of that, this document reads it epistemically as a process in which a pre-label possibility field irreversibly falls into a result label.

The claim is not “there is no physical collapse.” The claim is: in addition to the physical description, it can be read as irreversibility of labeling.

6. Observer

The observer is not an initially existing subject.

After an irreversible labeling relation is established, the name “observer” is retrospectively attached to one side of that relation.

This reversal clarifies the pseudo-problem of the von Neumann chain.

The chain is a propagation of labeling delay. The point of collapse is not an entity-location to be searched for; it is retrospectively fixed as the point where labeling becomes irreversible.

The observer does not produce observation. After the observation relation is established, one side is called the observer.

7. Residual e*

Violations of Bell inequalities and the Kochen-Specker theorem can be read as representative signs that quantum structure cannot be fully compressed into a classical label-space.

On the Y-axis side, this incompressibility is positioned as the place where residual e* appears.

This is not a claim to have solved those theorems.

It is a placement: the “something that cannot be collapsed into classical label-space” indicated by those results is read, from Pre-Label Geometry, as a thorn.

13.2 Born Rule: Projection Weight into Candidate Floors

The wavefunction \(\psi\) is a pre-label complex phase-structure. As such, it can include negative and imaginary components, and therefore cannot directly serve as a probability or as the weight of an exclusive fact-candidate for a macroscopic reference system.

The measurement basis \(|i\rangle\) can be read as an exclusive band that may become a floor of meaning for a macroscopic reference system.

The standard weight of candidate \(i\) is:

\[P_i = |\langle i|\psi\rangle|^2\]

In Y-axis terms, this is not a capricious probabilistic jump. It is the geometric projection-weight by which a pre-label complex phase-structure is projected into a candidate floor of meaning for a macroscopic reference system.

Under Pre-Label Geometry, the Born rule is supported in three directions and two layers.

Static support 1: Gleason-type support

When a separable Hilbert space has dimension at least three, a measure that consistently assigns non-negative, additive, normalized weights to exclusive floor-candidates is represented as a Born-type measure derived from a density operator. This is the content of Gleason's theorem. On the Y-axis side, this supports the formal consistency of floor-candidate weights.

Static support 2: POVM-type support

Actual measurement is not limited to ideal projective measurement. It includes apparatus, environment, coarse-graining, noise, and access constraints. A POVM describes the actual “window of candidate floors” available to a macroscopic reference system. It extends floor-candidate weighting into real measurement conditions.

Dynamic support: decision-theoretic support

When a reference system \(\mathcal{R}\) faces multiple exclusive candidate floors, if it uses weights deviating from the Born rule to make future expectations, actions, and updates, it falls outside the consistency conditions of rational decision and cannot stably maintain itself as a single reference system across future updates. Deutsch-Wallace-type decision-theoretic arguments support this dynamic consistency aspect.

Born rule stands statically as the formal consistency of exclusive, non-negative, additive candidate-floor weights, and dynamically as the consistency condition by which a reference system can continue future expectation, action, and update without breakdown.

13.3 Single Outcome: Phase Fixation into One Floor

To use multiple mutually exclusive floors as starting points at the same time is like trying to step both right and left with one foot.

For a macroscopic reference system to maintain its own coherence and continue subsequent updates, it must treat one of the mutually exclusive candidate floors as its starting point.

A single outcome does not mean that the world ontologically collapsed into one. It means that, for that reference system, the floor usable for subsequent coherent updates has phase-fixed into one.

This reading does not make a final ruling between Many-Worlds, objective collapse, QBism, or other interpretations. Before ontology, it places the epistemic condition that the reference system must use one floor of meaning.

13.4 Record: Irrecoverabilized Difference

A record does not primarily mean paper, data, or a representation inside consciousness.

More fundamentally, a record is the state in which a difference generated by interaction between system and measuring device has been redundantly diffused into environmental degrees of freedom and can no longer be recovered, by a macroscopic reference system, as a re-interferable pre-label structure.

\[\Delta_{\mathrm{int}} \xrightarrow{\;\mathrm{redundant\ diffusion\ into\ environmental\ degrees\ of\ freedom}\;} \Delta_{\mathrm{irrecoverable}} \equiv \mathrm{record}\]

A record is an irrecoverabilized difference.

This irreversibility does not mean that information absolutely disappears at the level of the whole universe. If one ideally treats a closed total system, information may remain preserved under unitary evolution.

However, once the replicas of that difference sink into inaccessible environmental degrees of freedom for a macroscopic reference system and are discarded by coarse-graining, it becomes effectively equivalent to being lost for that reference system.

13.5 Objectivity: Redundancy Plateau and Double Invariance

13.5.1 Meaning of the redundancy plateau

In quantum Darwinism, after interaction between system \(\mathcal{S}\) and environment \(\mathcal{E}\), the mutual information \(I(\mathcal{S}:\mathcal{F})\) held by an environmental fragment \(\mathcal{F}\) reaches a plateau early as fragment size increases.

Under Pre-Label Geometry, this is read as window invariance. Window invariance means that, for a single reference system \(\mathcal{R}\), no matter which environmental fragment \(\mathcal{F}_i\) is chosen as the window, the floor of meaning standing on the reference projection plane does not change. The plateau width is the size of the region in which this window invariance holds.

13.5.2 From window invariance to reference-system invariance

Window invariance alone is not enough for objectivity. For objectivity to stand, different reference systems \(\mathcal{R}_1, \mathcal{R}_2\), even when accessing different environmental fragments, must acknowledge isomorphic floors on their respective reference projection planes.

Here a strict distinction is necessary. The floor \(A_{\mathcal{R}_1}\) on \(\mathcal{R}_1\)'s plane and the floor \(A_{\mathcal{R}_2}\) on \(\mathcal{R}_2\)'s plane are not asserted to be ontologically identical from the beginning. What can first be said is that they are isomorphic.

\[A_{\mathcal{R}_1} \simeq A_{\mathcal{R}_2}\]

This isomorphism is materially supported by the redundant copying of the initial interaction-difference into multiple environmental fragments.

13.5.3 Definition of objectivity

Objectivity is the size of the region in which the double structure of window invariance and reference-system invariance holds at the same time.

What we call an objective fact is not an ontological attribute of the world. It is established when isomorphic floors, materially supported by environmental redundancy, are identified as the same through subsequent communication, comparison, and shared update.

13.5.4 The third-viewpoint problem

When floors acknowledged by different reference systems are judged to be “isomorphic” or “the same fact,” where does that comparison take place?

Comparing them requires a third operation that can look across both projection planes.

This section does not claim to have solved the third-viewpoint problem. It establishes the material ground of objectivity: environmental redundancy can generate isomorphic floors across distinct reference systems. How those floors are identified as the same fact within a shared world remains as the geometry of intersubjectivity.

13.5.5 Core

Objectivity is not a completed past that exists from the beginning. It is the size of the region in which redundant environmental copying allows isomorphic floors of meaning to stand, robust against both window changes within a single reference system and window changes across multiple reference systems. When those isomorphic floors are later identified through comparison, we call it an objective fact.

13.6 Strict Stratification of Unitary Evolution and Irreversible Labeling

13.6.0 Reconstruction

The previous 13.6 placed unitary evolution and irreversible labeling side by side as “material level” and “meaning level.” The direction was correct, but the word “level” could make them look like an upper/lower hierarchy, and the word “meaning” could make the latter look like subjective choice by a reference system. This section reconstructs them as two projection planes.

13.6.1 Two projection planes

Let the world be one closed total system \(\mathcal{W}\), and represent its state by the density operator \(\rho_{\mathcal{W}}\). Time evolution in the total-system description follows a complete unitary operator \(\hat{U}(t)\) satisfying information preservation.

The material projection plane (A-plane) is the descriptive plane that keeps all degrees of freedom of \(\mathcal{W}\) without discarding any of them. On this plane, correlations are not lost, phase is preserved, and evolution is strictly unitary.

\[\rho_{\mathcal{W}}(t) = \hat{U}(t) \rho_{\mathcal{W}}(0) \hat{U}^\dagger(t)\]

No finite reference system can observe this plane as direct experience.

The reference projection plane (B-plane) is the descriptive plane reduced to the subset of degrees of freedom accessible to a finite reference system \(\mathcal{R}\), denoted \(\mathcal{A}_{\mathcal{R}} \subset \mathcal{W}\). The inaccessible complement \(\mathcal{A}_{\mathcal{R}}^c\) is eliminated by partial trace.

\[\rho_{\mathcal{R}}(t) = \mathrm{Tr}_{\mathcal{A}_{\mathcal{R}}^c}\left[\hat{U}(t) \rho_{\mathcal{W}}(0) \hat{U}^\dagger(t)\right]\]

On this plane, interference terms are effectively suppressed in the pointer basis stabilized by environmental interaction. Evolution is generally non-unitary.

Here “orthogonal” does not mean Hilbert-space orthogonality. It means that the premises of the questions are mutually exclusive and cannot be directly compared on a single logical plane. The A-plane asks under no access restriction. The B-plane asks under access restriction.

13.6.2 Asymmetry of the mapping

The A-plane and B-plane do not form an upper/lower hierarchy. They are connected by a one-way mapping from A to B through partial trace.

\[\text{A} \xrightarrow{\;\mathrm{Tr}_{\mathcal{A}_{\mathcal{R}}^c}\;} \text{B}\]

The inverse mapping generally does not exist.

Information can remain preserved in the total system (A-plane) while appearing, for a finite reference system (B-plane), as an effectively irrecoverable record-difference. This one-way asymmetry is the geometric reason why unitary evolution and irreversible labeling can stand together without contradiction.

13.6.3 Coarse-graining as a constitutive condition of a reference system

Partial trace, or coarse-graining, is not a practical strategy chosen because of limited computational resources.

For a reference system \(\mathcal{R}\) to stand as a distinguished reference point inside the world \(\mathcal{W}\), it must have inaccessible degrees of freedom: \(\mathcal{A}_{\mathcal{R}}^c \neq \emptyset\). A system with access to all degrees of freedom is no longer a reference system observing the world; it is the limiting total-system description.

Having a coarse-grained boundary is itself the constitutive condition by which a reference system stands as a reference system.

13.6.4 Conditions for irreversibility

Partial trace is the descriptive form by which irreversibility appears on the B-plane. Irreversibility itself is grounded in a material event: the interaction-difference is redundantly diffused into a large-degree environment and the possibility of re-interference is statistically and effectively lost.

Partial trace: descriptive form of irreversibility.

Environmental diffusion, redundancy, decoherence: conditions for the establishment of irreversibility.

These belong to different layers of description.

13.6.5 Core

Unitary evolution and irreversible labeling are structures cut from the same total system by two orthogonal questions. They are connected by the one-way A→B mapping of partial trace. Because the inverse mapping generally does not exist, information can remain preserved in the total system while appearing to a finite reference system as an effectively irrecoverable record-difference. Coarse-graining is not negligence; it is the constitutive condition by which a reference system stands as a reference system.

13.7 Physical Time and the Past as Meaning

13.7.1 Time as two projection planes

Pre-Label Geometry does not deny thermodynamic or causal time. What it denies is the naive realism in which every point of the physical time series is already filled with completed facts.

The progression of time is redefined through two orthogonal projection planes.

The physical time-series plane describes the continuous order in which interaction-differences diffuse into environmental degrees of freedom. Difference is being carried, but the discrete meaning of “what happened” is not yet fixed. Physical time is the vehicle that carries difference.

The meaning-past plane is the discrete descriptive plane in which a later reference system adopts the diffused difference as a floor of meaning and thereby retrospectively fixes a past label.

13.7.2 Retrospective establishment from isomorphic floors to past labels

As shown in 13.5, environmental redundancy guarantees that isomorphic floors \(A_{\mathcal{R}_1} \simeq A_{\mathcal{R}_2}\) can arise on distinct reference systems' planes.

On the physical time-series plane, these floors remain individual shadows. They become a single objective past label only after communication, comparison, and shared update perform an effective identification operation.

13.7.3 Dissolving the time-series confusion

The question “if the time series exists, why is the past retrospective?” confuses the order in which difference is carried with the order in which that difference is acknowledged as a floor.

What a clock marks is continuity of motion, not fixation of fact.

13.7.4 Core

Physical time is the vehicle that continuously diffuses difference. The past as meaning is a record-label that arises retrospectively only when later reference systems compare and identify isomorphic floors produced by environmental redundancy, and adopt them as starting points for their own structures.

13.8 Observer

13.8.0 Reconstruction

The previous 13.8 said that an observer is the role-name attached to one side of an irreversible labeling relation after that relation is established. This order reversal remains correct. But after 13.6.3 rigorously defined the reference system as a structural concept, the relation between “reference system” and “observer” requires a sharper reconstruction.

13.8.1 Two descriptive dimensions

The event called “observation” has two descriptive dimensions.

The first is the structural description. Inside \(\mathcal{W}\), a boundary is drawn between accessible degrees of freedom \(\mathcal{A}_{\mathcal{R}}\) and inaccessible degrees of freedom \(\mathcal{A}_{\mathcal{R}}^c\), and a reference projection plane stands.

The second is the naming description. When a floor is acknowledged on that reference projection plane, the name “observer” is attached to one side of the acknowledgment relation, and the name “observed object” to the other.

These are not two different events. They are the structural side and the naming side of one event.

The observer does not possess a reference system. A reference-system structure stands, and the name “observer” is attached to a particular side of that structure.

13.8.3 Reorganizing the von Neumann chain

In the chain “apparatus → photon → retina → brain,” different reference projection planes stand at different stages. When redundant environmental diffusion has proceeded sufficiently and an irrecoverable floor-acknowledgment is established, the name “observer” is attached to one side of that acknowledgment relation.

The question is not “where is the observer?” but “on which reference projection plane did floor-acknowledgment occur?”

13.8.4 Reorganizing Wigner's Friend

This section does not presuppose friend and Wigner as two independent observer-subjects. It first reads them as two reference projection planes.

On the friend's reference projection plane, floor-acknowledgment has completed. On Wigner's reference projection plane, floor-acknowledgment has not yet been established, and the system including the friend is still held as an unlabeled structure.

When Wigner interacts with the friend, a new acknowledgment occurs on Wigner's plane. The two floors become isomorphic, pass through communication and comparison, and are identified as a shared objective fact.

There are not two objective facts. There are two reference projection planes, with different timings of acknowledgment.

13.8.5 Core

The observer is not the subject who first determines the world. It is the name attached to one side of an acknowledgment relation when floor-acknowledgment is established on a reference projection plane. The standing of a reference-system structure and the attachment of the observer-name are the structural side and naming side of the same event.
